About ME

My name is Yafet Tesfaye, and I was born in the vibrant city of Addis Ababa, the capital of Ethiopia. Addis Ababa, known as the political and cultural hub of the country, offers a rich tapestry of history, traditions, and a unique blend of urban and natural landscapes. Growing up in such a dynamic city has deeply influenced who I am today. I was born at Gandi Hospital and raised in Kazanchis Village until I was 8 years old. After that, my family moved to our current location in Lafto.

The bustling markets and historical sites, as well as the diverse communities and breathtaking views of the Entoto Mountains, have always been a source of inspiration and pride for me. The essence of Addis Ababa, with its mix of ancient heritage and modern development, has shaped my perspectives, values, and aspirations in life. Currently, I am 14 years old and studying in the 9th grade at Don Bosco Catholic School. This school has provided me with a solid educational foundation and a supportive community that encourages my growth and learning. The experiences and opportunities I have had in Addis Ababa and at my school have played a significant role in shaping the person I am becoming.

Growing up, I was always fascinated by the stories my grandparents would tell me about the rich history and culture of Ethiopia. My grandfather, who was a historian, often shared tales of ancient kingdoms, heroic battles, and the significant cultural shifts that have shaped our nation. One story that particularly captivated me was about the ancient city of Aksum, known for its towering obelisks and as the purported resting place of the Ark of the Covenant. These stories not only ignited my curiosity about our past but also instilled in me a deep sense of pride and responsibility to contribute to our future.

In Kazanchis Village, my early childhood was filled with vivid memories of playing with my friends in the narrow streets, exploring the vibrant markets, and attending the local community events that brought everyone together. Our village was a close-knit community where everyone knew each other, and there was a strong sense of solidarity and mutual support. I remember the local celebrations, especially during Meskel, the Ethiopian Orthodox holiday that commemorates the discovery of the True Cross by Queen Helena. The village would come alive with colorful processions, traditional dances, and the lighting of the Damera bonfire, which symbolized the triumph of light over darkness.

When I was 8 years old, my family moved to Lafto, a district that offered new experiences and opportunities. Lafto, with its more modern infrastructure and diverse population, presented a different facet of Addis Ababa. The move was both exciting and challenging as I had to adapt to a new environment, make new friends, and get accustomed to a different pace of life. However, this transition taught me resilience and adaptability, qualities that have been invaluable in my personal growth.

One of the most influential periods of my life has been my time at Don Bosco Catholic School. This institution is renowned not only for its academic excellence but also for its emphasis on holistic development, moral values, and community service. The school's philosophy of nurturing compassionate and responsible individuals resonated deeply with me. At Don Bosco, I have had the opportunity to engage in various extracurricular activities, from joining the debate club to participating in science fairs and volunteering for community service projects.

The debate club, in particular, has been a significant part of my journey at Don Bosco. It has honed my public speaking skills, taught me the importance of critical thinking, and helped me develop the ability to view issues from multiple perspectives. Participating in regional and national debate competitions has broadened my horizons and given me the confidence to express my ideas effectively. These experiences have also made me more aware of the pressing social and political issues facing our society, inspiring me to be an active and informed citizen.

In addition to the debate club, I have been actively involved in the school's science club. My fascination with science and technology has driven me to explore various fields, from robotics to environmental science. I remember our project on sustainable energy solutions, where we researched and developed a prototype of a solar-powered water purification system. This project not only deepened my understanding of renewable energy but also highlighted the importance of innovation in addressing real-world problems. It was incredibly fulfilling to see our project recognized at the national science fair, and it reinforced my passion for pursuing a career in the STEM fields.

Volunteering has also been a cornerstone of my experience at Don Bosco. The school emphasizes the importance of giving back to the community, and I have participated in numerous initiatives, such as organizing book drives for underprivileged schools, participating in environmental clean-up campaigns, and assisting in local shelters. These activities have taught me the value of empathy, compassion, and the impact of collective action. One of the most memorable volunteering experiences was our visit to a rural village to set up a temporary library and conduct literacy workshops for the children. The joy and excitement on their faces as they explored the world of books were truly heartwarming and motivating.

Aside from my school activities, I have always been passionate about sports, particularly soccer. Playing soccer has not only been a source of physical fitness but also a way to build teamwork and leadership skills. I have been a member of our school's soccer team since I joined Don Bosco, and the camaraderie and discipline instilled by our coach have been invaluable. Competing in inter-school tournaments and representing our school at various levels has been a thrilling experience, teaching me the importance of perseverance, strategy, and sportsmanship.

Music has also been a significant part of my life. I grew up listening to traditional Ethiopian music, which has a unique blend of rhythmic patterns and melodies that reflect our cultural heritage. Inspired by this, I started learning to play the kirar, a traditional Ethiopian string instrument, at a young age. Over the years, I have also developed an interest in modern music and have taken piano lessons to broaden my musical repertoire. Music, for me, is a form of expression and a way to connect with my roots while also exploring contemporary genres.

Living in Addis Ababa has provided me with numerous opportunities to immerse myself in cultural activities and events. The city's vibrant arts scene, with its theaters, galleries, and festivals, has been a constant source of inspiration. I particularly enjoy visiting the National Museum, which houses some of the most significant artifacts of our country's history, including the famous fossil of Lucy, one of the earliest known hominids. These visits have deepened my appreciation for our rich cultural heritage and the importance of preserving it for future generations.

The annual Addis Ababa International Book Fair is another event I look forward to each year. This fair attracts authors, publishers, and readers from all over the world, providing a platform for literary exchange and the promotion of reading culture. Attending the fair has allowed me to meet some of my favorite authors, discover new books, and engage in stimulating discussions about literature and ideas. It has also inspired me to pursue writing, and I have contributed articles to our school magazine on topics ranging from science and technology to social issues and cultural events.

In addition to my academic and extracurricular pursuits, I have always been passionate about technology and its potential to transform lives. This interest led me to participate in a coding bootcamp organized by a local tech incubator. The bootcamp introduced me to various programming languages and equipped me with the skills to develop basic applications. One of the projects I worked on was a mobile app designed to connect volunteers with local community service opportunities. This project not only enhanced my coding skills but also underscored the importance of using technology for social good.

Looking to the future, I aspire to pursue a career in software development. The rapidly evolving world of technology has always fascinated me, and I am particularly interested in how software can be used to solve complex problems and improve people's lives. My goal is to become a proficient software developer who can create innovative solutions that address real-world challenges. Whether it's developing applications that enhance education, healthcare, or community engagement, I believe that software development offers limitless possibilities to make a positive impact.

In conclusion, my journey thus far has been shaped by the rich experiences and opportunities I have had growing up in Addis Ababa, studying at Don Bosco Catholic School, and engaging in various activities that have broadened my horizons. The vibrant culture, supportive community, and dynamic environment of my city have played a crucial role in molding my character and aspirations. As I continue to learn and grow, I am committed to making a positive impact on my community and beyond, driven by the values and passions that have been instilled in me.
